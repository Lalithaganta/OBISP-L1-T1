ABOUT US

The formation of a vegetable begins with a few cells responding to light signals, rapidly growing and establishing a plant base. As the root and stem tips elongate, the vegetable transitions from a seedling to a seedling plant. A meristematic region at the tip ensures ongoing growth and cell division, differentiating into specialized cells for transport and storage. Roots anchor the plant, providing stability, while leaf tips initiate photosynthesis. Further leaf unfurling creates a protective canopy, aided by a symbiotic relationship with microorganisms for nutrient uptake, contributing to the overall health and stability of the vegetable.
